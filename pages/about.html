About

After transferring from Baruch College to NYC College of Technology and ultimately to the Rochester Institute of Technology, I found my lane at the intersection of art, technology, and games. I graduated summa cum laude with a B.S. in Game Design and Development in December 2024.

Today I focus on gameplay programming in Unreal Engine, mostly C++ and Blueprints, and I've sharpened those skills through personal projects, an internship, and a contract role. I like work that makes a game meaningfully better: improving performance, building clean, scalable systems, and polishing features.

I enjoy collaborating closely with artists, animators, writers, and audio/VFX talent, because the best gameplay code supports their work as much as it supports mechanics.

Outside of work, I play volleyball, go to the gym, listen to audiobooks, and lately I've been playing Cult of the Lamb and Guild Wars 2.
